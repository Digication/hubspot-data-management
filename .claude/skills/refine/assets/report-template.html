The Resonance Mechanism

The most effective way to surface what a person knows is not to ask them, but to show them something concrete and let them react. When the AI is specifically wrong in a domain the human knows, the mismatch activates deep recall.

This is why the system shows concrete before/after proposals instead of asking open-ended questions. Your corrections carry far more information than unprompted answers — each "no, actually..." reveals constraints, facts, and preferences that improve subsequent cycles.

Context annotations are classified as:

Factual Domain knowledge surfaced by corrections
Constraint Boundaries and limitations revealed by rejections
Preference Style and tone signals from modifications

Why Scores Vary Between Steps

Each Discover and Measure phase uses a fresh, independent evaluator — like two different reviewers grading the same work. Scores may vary ±0.5–1.0 points between phases. The important number is the delta within each cycle, not the absolute score between cycles. When scores are ≥8.0, deltas within ±1.0 are treated as measurement noise.
